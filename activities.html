Introduction

Welcome to the photos page where I list a small collection of photos from my bicycle rides. Riding helps to keep me grounded and is a form of meditation, while riding through many neighborhoods around the city. I own two bicycles, one which I built from spare parts and the other is an e-bike. I get plenty of use from both. Before the pandemic I would average below 20 miles. But after, I average closer to 30 miles. The most I ever rode was 47 miles and that was to Rockland County and back from the Bronx.

On one December day, I was visiting a friend and on the news was a report about people in Wuhan, China getting sick. There were no alarm bells and I went about my routines and didn't think anything of it. January then February rolled around and the news got more urgent with regards to the virus spreading to other parts of the world and with Italy in the middle of a crisis. Nothing was being done here at home but I was beginning to get worried. At the end of February and the beginning of March, it was imminent that all that was going to change. Sure enough, by the middle of March, as the pandemic was declared, the centers where I taught would be closing. I was suddenly out of work. As the days went on, it got more bleek where even shopping for supplies was becoming an issue. Even seeing stores all around being closed and worried they would close for good. More importantly was the fear of getting sick. Later in the year, my mother would pass away but from a different ailment. The anxiety and uncertainty just kept piling up. And, I still wasn't making any money.

I needed something to help break the cycle and to overcome my anxiety so, I setup Sundays aside to go bicycle riding. Started with short trips at first but, I pushed myself to go further each time. I must first mention that I've bicycled alot in the past. In fact use to ride 14 miles per day to/from work while crossing the Manhattan Bridge but I haven't done that for awhile. Riding on Sundays helped to build up my endurance and more so as I got to explore more neighborhoods over different routes. On some Sundays I would go to Riverdale, Van Cortlandt Park, South Bronx, Soundview, Orchard Beach and City Island in the Bronx. Other days I would go up to Mount Vernon and New Rochelle. Trips became longer and longer visiting Yonkers, Manhattan, Queens, Westchester County and as far as Rockland County and New Jersey, wearing my mask the whole time.

I'm still riding consistently on Sundays, not missing a single week with the exception when it snowed heavly one Sunday. Just recently, I rode without a mask. In fact, discovered that I could go even further as if wearing the mask was a training tool. The best parts of the rides are visiting the different neighborhoods, breathing fresh air, and getting a good nights sleep at night. The photos I've posted here are just a few. If you want to hear more about my rides or would like to get into riding yourself and have questions, feel free to reach out to me.
